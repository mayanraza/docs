# Step 3: Greeting Builder

Customize your WhatsApp chatbot's welcome message and initial interaction. This is the first message users receive when they start a conversation with your business on WhatsApp.

---

## Overview

The Greeting Builder for WhatsApp allows you to create a welcoming first impression, add quick reply buttons, and configure essential business information that helps the chatbot assist users effectively.

## Customize Your Welcome Message

### Welcome Message Text
**Purpose:** The first message users receive when they message your WhatsApp number.

- Create an engaging, friendly greeting
- Example: "Hey üëã I'm here for you! I'm ChatGPT, and I can answer your questions about TechBackers and our services."
- Keep it conversational - WhatsApp is an informal platform
- Use emojis to add personality and warmth
- Clearly state what the chatbot can help with

**WhatsApp Best Practices:**
- Keep it under 2-3 sentences
- Be warm and welcoming
- Set clear expectations about what the bot can do
- Use a friendly, conversational tone

---

## Buttons

**Purpose:** Quick reply buttons that appear with the welcome message for instant navigation.

Add buttons for common user needs:

- **FAQ** - Frequently asked questions
- **About Us** - Company information
- **Contact Us** - How to reach you

**How to Configure:**
1. Click the **+** button to add more buttons
2. Each button triggers a specific response or flow
3. Limit to 3 buttons for WhatsApp (platform limitation)

**WhatsApp Button Guidelines:**
- Maximum 3 buttons per message
- Button text should be short (max 20 characters)
- Use action-oriented language ("Get Pricing", not "Pricing Information")

---

## Company Information

### Company Name
**Purpose:** Your business name as it appears in conversations.

- Enter your official business name
- Example: "TechBackers"
- This helps users confirm they're chatting with the right business
- Should match your WhatsApp Business Profile name

### Company Industry
**Purpose:** Your business sector for context-aware responses.

- Select from dropdown (e.g., "Healthcare & Life Sciences")
- Helps the AI understand industry-specific terminology
- Influences response style and recommendations
- Ensures appropriate tone for your sector

---

## Contact Information

### Contact Number
**Purpose:** Primary phone number for your business.

- Include country code (e.g., +92)
- Format: +92 - +1 444-567-6578
- This can be shared with users who need direct contact
- Should be a number that's actively monitored

### Contact Email
**Purpose:** Primary email for customer support.

- Use a monitored support email
- Example: support@techbackers.com
- The chatbot can share this for complex inquiries
- Useful for sending documents or detailed information

---

## Social Media Profiles

**Purpose:** Link other communication channels for multi-platform engagement.

- Click **Add Social Media** to include:
  - Facebook
  - Instagram
  - Twitter/X
  - LinkedIn
  - YouTube
- Provides users alternative ways to connect
- Builds brand presence across platforms

## Goodbye Message

**Purpose:** Friendly closing message when conversations end.

- Example: "If you need anything else, you can send a message anytime. I'm here to help!"
- Encourages users to return
- Reinforces 24/7 availability
- Leaves a positive impression

**WhatsApp Considerations:**
- WhatsApp conversations don't really "end" like website chats
- This message may appear after periods of inactivity
- Keep it short and welcoming for future messages

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

> [!WARNING]
> **Avoid These Common Errors:**

‚ùå **Too Long:** WhatsApp users expect quick, scannable messages
- **Instead:** Keep welcome message under 100 words

‚ùå **Too Formal:** WhatsApp is a casual platform
- **Instead:** Use conversational language and emojis

‚ùå **Unclear Purpose:** Users don't know what the bot can do
- **Instead:** Clearly list what the bot can help with

‚ùå **Too Many Buttons:** More than 3 buttons overwhelms users
- **Instead:** Stick to 3 most important options

‚ùå **Generic Message:** Doesn't reflect your brand
- **Instead:** Customize with your brand voice and personality

## Testing Your Greeting

Before finalizing:

1. **Read it aloud** - Does it sound natural?
2. **Check length** - Is it concise enough?
3. **Verify buttons** - Are they clear and actionable?
4. **Test emojis** - Do they render correctly?
5. **Brand alignment** - Does it match your brand voice?
